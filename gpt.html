Visits

The Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) in India is the nodal ministry for all matters concerning disaster management⁵. The Disaster Management Division within the MHA is responsible for response, relief, and preparedness for natural calamities and man-made disasters². This includes conducting visits to disaster-stricken areas to assess the situation and coordinate relief operations³. One of the key documents that guide these efforts is the National Disaster Management Plan (NDMP), which was published by the National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) under the MHA¹. The NDMP provides a framework for disaster management in India and aligns with the Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction 2015-2030¹. In terms of international involvement, there are several organizations and countries that collaborate with India during disaster management operations. For instance, during the 2015 Nepal earthquake, India launched Operation Maitri, deploying the Indian Army, Air Force, and other specialized teams. This operation was recognized by a top UN official who acknowledged India's role as a "first responder"⁶. Moreover, NGOs like CARE India also play a significant role in disaster management. They carry out annual risk profiling and mapping of vulnerable locations in India to enhance preparedness to respond during emergencies⁸. However, it's important to note that the specifics of these visits and collaborations can vary based on the nature of the disaster and the requirements of the situation.
